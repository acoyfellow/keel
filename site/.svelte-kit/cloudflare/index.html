Artifact-bound completion gate

Software does not get to call itself done.

Keel admits a new version only when a signed proof binds observed behavior to the exact candidate artifact. Promotion is compare-and-swap. Refusal rolls back to known-good. The trail is signed and hash-chained.

Mechanism

The gate is small enough to audit.

The load-bearing primitive is content addressing. A proof, a promotion, and a rollback all name the same bytes.

01

A candidate is named by content digest.

02

A verifier builds, smokes, and records evidence for that exact digest.

03

The proof is signed by an active trusted key, or by enough keys for a threshold.

04

Promotion uses a scoped token and compare-and-swap against the known baseline.

05

Every decision appends to a hash-chained receipt.

Boundary

What Keel does not own.

The library is intentionally provider-agnostic. Callers supply the deploy, verify, rollback, and storage ports.

  • Keel does not deploy an app for you.
  • Keel does not hold long-lived write credentials.
  • Keel does not trust a passing test without an artifact-bound proof.
  • Keel does not claim downstream adoption until another project imports it and deletes code.
